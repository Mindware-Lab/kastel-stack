# Zapier (automation + agents)

## Snapshot
Zapier is a mainstream automation platform with huge connector coverage and increasingly "agentic" workflows.

## Where it overlaps with Kastel Stack
- App-to-app automations and triggers.
- Helps small teams automate lead follow-ups, CRM updates, and admin tasks quickly.
- Can provide fast "time-to-value" during Tier A prototyping.

## Where Zapier is strong
- Breadth of integrations and fast setup.
- Mature product experience for non-technical users.
- Strong "automation as a habit" for small businesses.

## What Zapier tends to lack (relative to Kastel Stack)
- **Sovereign/ownable mode** is not the core offer.
- The platform is optimised for automation coverage, not an explicit **safety gate** with proof checks as the default lifecycle.
- "Exit-by-design" is weaker: you can export logic conceptually, but not in a way that guarantees continuity without the platform.
- Tier C/D concerns (local inference, local audit, strict comms/docs boundary) are outside the normal Zapier posture.

## Kastel Stack positioning (short)
Kastel Stack is "automation you can delegate" with explicit governance:
**Auto-run / Draft-for-approval / Escalate**, immutable audit, rollback, and a sovereign appliance option.

## Partner / integration angle
- Use Zapier as the quick connector layer in Tier A while proving workflows.
- Replace critical paths with hardened internal services as you progress to Tier B/C.

## Questions to verify
- Can we enforce approval gates and immutable auditing across all high-risk actions?
- How portable are workflows and logs if a customer must exit quickly?
